## Style Guide Improvements

- Use restrained editorial hierarchy rather than decorative startup spectacle.
- Prioritize real program, location, founder, event, and partner evidence over abstract innovation claims.
- Let RTIH's hub-and-spoke model become the core visual and information architecture device.
- Use motion only to clarify transitions, reveal density, or show ecosystem relationships.
- Keep visual sophistication tied to trust: clean hierarchy, strong scanning, clear routes, and real proof.

## Typography Improvements

- Favor durable editorial typography that can support long institutional pages, program explainers, founder stories, and event archives.
- Avoid oversized display typography inside operational sections.
- Use type scale to distinguish audience pathways, proof points, program details, and institutional context.
- Make mobile headings compact enough to preserve meaning without crowding.

## CTA System Improvements

- Replace generic single-path CTAs with audience-routed CTAs.
- Primary founder CTAs should answer: what program fits me, what is open now, what evidence do I need, and what happens after I apply.
- Partner CTAs should separate government, university, corporate, mentor, investor, and media intent.
- Event CTAs should distinguish register, view agenda, submit challenge, partner with event, and see outcomes.

## Founder Funnel Improvements

- Founder discovers RTIH through sector/program/event/startup evidence.
- Founder identifies fit by stage, sector, location, and support type.
- Founder sees credible proof: mentors, labs, programs, outcomes, events, and real startup examples.
- Founder chooses a clear next action without decoding institutional structure.
- Founder receives a pathway that feels supportive rather than bureaucratic.

## Partner Funnel Improvements

- Partner first understands RTIH's Andhra Pradesh ecosystem role.
- Partner chooses a collaboration model: program, challenge, lab, mentorship, funding, university, government, or event.
- Partner sees institutional credibility and operational clarity before a contact CTA.
- Partner contact flows should ask enough to route properly without becoming a procurement form.

## Event Storytelling Improvements

- Events should be treated as ecosystem evidence, not just announcements.
- Event cards need date, location, audience, theme, host/partner, participation CTA, and outcome after completion.
- Past events should build trust through photos, summaries, participant mix, and follow-on opportunities.

## Ecosystem Communication Improvements

- RTIH should explain its ecosystem through locations, sectors, programs, people, partners, events, and startup journeys.
- Avoid generic "innovation hub" phrasing unless paired with concrete mechanisms.
- Make Visakhapatnam and Andhra Pradesh context visible without making the site feel locally constrained.
- The ecosystem map should help users navigate, not decorate the page.

## Rejection Rules

- Reject patterns that depend on fake metrics, abstract visuals, or generic startup energy.
- Reject interfaces that look premium but make founder or partner routing slower.
- Reject experimental layouts that weaken accessibility or mobile comprehension.
- Reject trend-led motion unless it solves hierarchy, density, or orientation.
